Recovery Guides & Tips

Official steps to recover your Gmail and Instagram, plus expert tips.

  1. Visit accounts.google.com/signin/recovery in your browser
  2. Enter your Gmail address and click Next
  3. Click "Try a different way" to see all recovery options
  4. If you had a linked phone number, Google can send a code via SMS
  5. If you have a trusted device (phone/laptop) still logged in, confirm there
  6. If none of those work, answer the security questions Google asks (approximate creation date, recovery email, etc.)
  7. As a last resort, try the passwords generated above one by one in the standard login

💡 Tip: Try recovery from a device and network you normally use — Google uses this to verify identity.

  1. Open Instagram app → tap "Get more help" below the login form
  2. Enter your username, email, or phone number linked to the account
  3. If you have access to the linked email/phone, choose that option and confirm the code
  4. If not, tap "I can't access this email or phone number"
  5. Instagram may offer a video selfie verification — this compares your face to tagged photos on the account
  6. If you have Facebook linked, you may be able to log in via Facebook
  7. Try the generated passwords manually via the normal Instagram login

💡 Tip: The video selfie option has the highest success rate if your face appears in account photos.

Most people create passwords using a small set of personal patterns — a familiar name, a meaningful date, and a simple suffix like "123" or "!". Over time we forget which exact combination we used for which account, especially if we had to change it at some point. Pass Predictor reconstructs all realistic variants of your patterns, ranked by how commonly people build passwords.
